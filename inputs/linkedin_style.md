## Sample 4 — Transition journey (Summer of Product S2)

"Isn't Product Management simply managing products from start the finish?"  

This was actually a stereotype I had in mind since the time I first heard about these two words.   

Oh I was so wrong!  

In session 2 of "Summer of Product" hosted by **[Ankit Shukla](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ankythshukla/)** from **[HelloPM](https://www.linkedin.com/company/hello-pm/)** all my conventional thoughts were shattered.  

Watch this: **[https://lnkd.in/gBXpG3Ee](https://lnkd.in/gBXpG3Ee)**  

Let me break it down for you:  

1. One cannot become a PM by simply gathering knowledge. Practice makes a man/woman perfect.
2. But there must be enough substance to show as well to support the numerous practice sessions and acquired knowledge.
3. Only passion is not enough to get into this role. But passion with proof can definitely help deliver the expected outcomes.
4. AI can be both a friend and a foe. It's wise use can help do wonders in this field.
5. Thorough market research a specific industry is very important to break into one. Continuous market and competitor research should however follow to be up to date.
6. Product teardowns need to be a regular practice, in order to be able to start thinking like a seasoned Product Manager. It is a reverse engineering exercise that helps one understand what the PM was thinking while building that particular product.
7. Most of us have, at one point in life, craved for atleast one particular feature in a particular product. PMs take such opportunities and turn them into Product Improvement case studies and projects.
8. Side projects are useful because it helps a PM understand from perspectives of a Business Owner, stakeholder, end-user, developer and obviously as a PM. A PM should always have a side project of their own. This ensures continuous evaluation and training of the mind.
9. One thing is for sure that a PMs role is a little bit of everything. Therefore, no matter what role an aspirant is from, it is possible to transition because they already know a part of it.
10. Basically, a PM is and needs to be and think like a PM both in and outside the job as well. Only then can an aspirant expect to be ready for any kind of problem put in front of them and prove to be a worthy asset.

In conclusion, the answer to the question (thought) above is "YES", this job IS about managing products end-to-end. But "there is a lot more Nitty-Gritty in this job than meets the eye".

## Sample 5 — AI-assisted PM (product thinking / MVP)

💡 “I thought my idea was unique, but similar products already exist in the market.” 💡  

This is a thought almost every new and seasoned Product Manager has when trying to build something from scratch.  

And honestly? It shouldn’t discourage you.  

There’s just one question that truly matters while building or managing any product:  

👉 "Does this product actually solve a user problem?"  

Let’s break down what helps answer that question:  

1. PMs who think in terms of user value (not just features) clearly demonstrate strong PRODUCT THINKING.
2. The moment you shift focus from what the product does to how the user experiences it, value creation becomes inevitable.
3. When Product Thinking is combined with the OUTCOME v/s OUTPUT mindset, products naturally stand out.
4. This approach helps decide whether a feature is needed based on results and not functionality.
5. MVP (Minimum Viable Product) - a powerful way to test desirability, usability, feasibility, and viability early.
6. MVPs are often misunderstood as a “feature-packed smaller version” of the final product. In reality, they are the simplest possible version built to validate whether the problem is worth solving.
7. All of this comes together through the BUILD-MEASURE-LEARN loop.
8. You BUILD, MEASURE outcomes, LEARN what works, and iterate - continuously. Product building is never linear; it’s a closed feedback loop.

So does it matter if a similar product already exists?  
Not really.  

Building from scratch teaches you customer-centricity, and once a PM truly understands how to create user value, failure stops being the end—it becomes feedback.

## Sample 6 — AI-assisted PM (Groww RAG build)

Look What I Built! My First RAG Chatbot and I am calling it "**[Groww](https://www.linkedin.com/company/groww.in/)** Fund **[Gyaan.AI](http://Gyaan.AI)**".  

Really not sure how I can show my excitement and satisfaction via this post but I am sure you will get it.  

Let me tell you how it is wired in the backend:  
👉 Can ingest all Groww mutual funds (currently only 8 ingested)  
👉 Scrapes all the ingested funds every day without fail at 9:45 AM  
👉 Pulls all the data, cleans and parses relevant ones properly  
👉 Reads the data thoroughly and create relevant data chunks  
👉 Uses "bge-small-en" model to create embeddings of these chunks  
👉 Stores these chunks in a vector database called "Chroma DB"  

And when a user puts in a query for a specific fund from the frontend:  
👉 Data retrieval (from vector database) process starts  
👉 Chunks are pulled in and top-k ranked  
👉 Relevant chunks are then added on top of the user query  
👉 This entire package is then provided to my Groq LLM as a prompt  
👉 Which then replies in 2 exact sentences and the fund link as citation  

User Problem Solved: Getting trustworthy mutual fund facts from Groww mutual fund pages all in one place. Facts only, Nothing Generic.
