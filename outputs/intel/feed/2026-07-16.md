#### The same technique that had Tom Brady playing football at 41 can fix your painful elbows. We've all felt it. Sitting at our desks, gripping a mouse, lifting at the gym, slowly destroying our elbows. Brady spent 10 years trying to solve his with ice. It never worked. It won't work for you either. What actually works is manual therapy: deep, direct muscle work that lengthens and softens the forearm, bicep, and tricep. Not massage for relaxation. Firm pressure along the muscle while you slowly move the joint, dropping the resting tension in everything that pulls on the elbow. Here's why it works. When your tendon hurts, you have a mild grade of tendinitis, which literally means inflamed tendon. But when researchers biopsied painful tendons, they didn't find inflammation. They found frayed, disorganized collagen. Your tendon is degenerating, not swelling. So icing does nothing, because there's no fire to put out. You need to treat the fraying. A tendon is a rope tying muscle to bone, and when the muscles stay tight, the rope stays cranked even at rest, tugging the frayed spot with every rep. Manual therapy releases the muscles, the tension on the tendon drops, and the fraying finally gets a chance to remodel. That's why Brady's decade of pain resolved in 5 days. So when you hear about pliability and Tom Brady's longevity, this is what it really is. Softer muscles, less tension on the tendon, no more tugging on damaged tissue. He used it to throw a football until 45. You can use it on your elbows starting this week.

#### Christopher Nolan's $250 million movie depends on one building in Burbank, 41 theaters worldwide, and roughly 90 people on Earth who know how to run the projector. The Odyssey is the first feature shot entirely on IMAX film, and FotoKem is the last lab anywhere that can make the 70mm prints. Every one of the thousands of cuts in the film was spliced by hand with a glue pot. The production burned through 2.1 million feet of negative, about 400 miles of film, and every foot of it flew back to that single lab for processing. Each finished print costs around $80,000 to make. One Las Vegas theater spent $32,000 on humidifiers just to keep its copy from drying out. Its projector had sat idle since Interstellar in 2014. Here's what the scarcity bought: when 70mm tickets went on sale a full year before release, 95% of seats sold within an hour. Retail price was $25 to $28. Resellers got $300 to $400. Presales have already broken the all-time IMAX record. Studios spent 20 years abandoning film because digital was cheaper to ship. Nolan kept the last lab alive and turned the bottleneck itself into the marketing. Thousands of rooms can play the 4K version. Only 41 can show the 18K version, and that gap is why people are crossing state lines for a movie ticket. The format Hollywood declared dead in 2010 is now the scarcest luxury good in entertainment.
