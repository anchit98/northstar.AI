## 1. Core Positioning Statement
**"The Execution-Obsessed, AI-Native Product Builder."**

You are not a traditional, theoretical PM who only writes PRDs and waits for engineering. You are a **Product Manager** (not a BA) — early in the PM journey but already shipping. You own **product discovery**, **product strategy**, **roadmap prioritization (RICE)**, and **end-to-end delivery**—from **MVP** to **UAT** and **adoption**. You track **KPI ownership** (adoption, retention, churn, CSAT), run **data-driven prioritization**, and ship **AI-native products** using Cursor, **LLM integration**, and **prompt engineering**. You bridge **cross-functional leadership**, **product operations**, and technical execution.

**Positioning gap to close on LinkedIn:** Readers may still see you as a Business Analyst. Posts should naturally signal **PM ownership** (discovery, prioritization, shipping, adoption) without sounding defensive.

## 5. Interview Introductions

> **Use:** Pick the script that matches the role (see `analysis/positioning_strategy.md` variants A/B/C). Aim for **60–90 seconds** (~150–220 words), then pause for follow-ups.
>
> **Structure:** Present → Past proof → Why this role → Close with a question.

### Variant A — AI-Native Builder PM

**Best for:** AI startups, Series A–B, founder-led hiring, “technical PM” roles.

**Lead with:** Shipped AI products (RAG chatbot, Review Analyzer agent), LLM-assisted delivery, builder mindset.

```text
Hi, I’m Anchit — I’m an AI-native product manager who ships end-to-end, not just documents.

Most recently at WPP Media I’ve operated like an internal PM: discovery, prioritization, MVP, and adoption. My title says Business Analyst, but the work is product ownership — I’ve run 200+ discovery cycles, said no with data, and kept stakeholders aligned on what actually moves revenue and hours saved.

What differentiates me is I build. I shipped a mutual fund RAG chatbot — daily ingestion, ChromaDB, Groq with citations — used by 200+ people with sub-three-second responses. I also built an agentic Review Analyzer for app-store VoC: weekly pipelines, MCP into Docs and email, leadership-ready summaries without manual assembly. I use Cursor and LLMs to go from ambiguity to working software fast — like a one-day reporting MVP that grew into an RLS chatbot once we validated the UX.

I’m looking for a PM role where I can own AI-native product outcomes — discovery through adoption — in a team that values speed and measurable impact. I’d love to hear what you’re building next and where you want a PM who can both prioritize and ship.
```

### Variant B — Product Operations & Scale PM

**Best for:** Series B/C, platform or product ops roles, internal tooling at scale.

**Lead with:** Intake systems, cycle-time reduction, cross-functional delivery, operational rigor.

```text
Hi, I’m Anchit — I’m a product operator who turns messy demand into shipped, adopted internal products.

At WPP Media I sit at the center of automation and AI product requests across India. I don’t just take orders — I run structured discovery, prioritize with a data-backed model (effort, savings, KPI fit), and align stakeholders before engineering starts. That discipline across 200+ initiatives is how we kept prioritization objective and tied to real business outcomes, not feature volume.

On delivery, I’m known for phased roadmaps under ambiguity. On Meta campaign automation — high stakes after manual errors — I scoped phases one and two for the highest-volume cases, hit the deadline, then expanded for edge cases with cross-functional alignment. Result: zero setup errors, full adoption, and roughly ₹1.5 Cr+ in department savings. I also care about adoption as a product metric: reach, engagement, and task completion — not “we launched it.” That’s how we drove strong adoption, retention, and CSAT on internal tools.

I’m targeting product operations or platform PM roles where scale, predictability, and stakeholder trust matter. I’d like to understand how your team intake works today and where you want more rigor without slowing the business down.
```

### Variant C — Growth & Analytics PM

**Best for:** Growth PM, product analytics, PLG, retention-focused startups.

**Lead with:** Adoption, retention, churn, CSAT, funnel thinking, GTM-adjacent background.

```text
Hi, I’m Anchit — I’m a growth-oriented product manager who measures success in adoption and outcomes, not launch dates.

My foundation is half GTM and half product: I started in sales and QA, so I think in funnels, friction, and what makes users actually change behavior. At WPP Media I own the full loop for internal products — discovery, ship, then prove value with a metrics stack: reach, engagement, and completion rates. We didn’t celebrate deployment; we tracked DAU, usage, and issues per product and iterated when adoption lagged.

Concrete examples: internal automation portfolio with adoption in the ~80% range, strong retention and CSAT — because we trained users and closed the loop on feedback quickly. On the AI side, a mutual fund facts chatbot with 200+ users taught me how trust and latency shape repeat usage. Externally, my Swish teardown series was classic growth PM work — VoC into personas, failure modes, and recovery flows — without pretending I had internal metrics I don’t own.

I’m looking for a growth or analytics-leaning PM role where I can own activation, retention, or core loop improvements with clear north-star metrics. What’s the one metric your product team is trying to move this quarter, and where do you feel the biggest leak in the funnel today?
```
