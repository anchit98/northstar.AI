# Personal Branding Recommendations

> **Date:** 2026-05-15 | **Updated:** 2026-05-23 (content pillars v2)
> **Target Audience:** Series B startup founders, product leadership, recruiters, hiring managers, talent acquisition, aspiring PMs, PM peers

---

## 1. Core Positioning Statement
**"The Execution-Obsessed, AI-Native Product Builder."**

You are not a traditional, theoretical PM who only writes PRDs and waits for engineering. You are a **Product Manager** (not a BA) — early in the PM journey but already shipping. You own **product discovery**, **product strategy**, **roadmap prioritization (RICE)**, and **end-to-end delivery**—from **MVP** to **UAT** and **adoption**. You track **KPI ownership** (adoption, retention, churn, CSAT), run **data-driven prioritization**, and ship **AI-native products** using Cursor, **LLM integration**, and **prompt engineering**. You bridge **cross-functional leadership**, **product operations**, and technical execution.

**Positioning gap to close on LinkedIn:** Readers may still see you as a Business Analyst. Posts should naturally signal **PM ownership** (discovery, prioritization, shipping, adoption) without sounding defensive.

## 2. LinkedIn Profile Optimization

### Visuals
- **Profile Photo:** Ensure your photo is high-quality, approachable, and professional but modern (no stiff corporate ties; think startup casual).
- **Background Banner:** Create a simple, clean banner (using Canva/Figma) with a tagline like: *"Building Scalable AI Products & Automation Platforms"* or *"Turning Ambiguity into Shipped Products."*

### Featured Section
Pin your best case studies and portfolio links to the top of your profile:
1. **Review Analyzer AI Agent (Groww)** (agentic VoC, weekly leadership summaries, MCP, Google Stitch)
2. **Meta Campaign Setup Automation** (Phased roadmap, ₹1.5 Cr+ impact, cross-functional delivery)
3. **Mutual Fund Factual Response RAG Chatbot** (scheduled ingestion, ChromaDB RAG, Groww-inspired UI, 200+ users)

## 3. Content Pillars (For LinkedIn Posting)

> **Intel & Content Engine (Phase 12):** `/workbench/intel/posts` reads this section + [`inputs/linkedin_style.md`](../inputs/linkedin_style.md). Prompt: [`prompts/intel_post.md`](../prompts/intel_post.md). IDs: `pillar-1` … `pillar-4` (must match `frontend/lib/intelPillars.ts`).
>
> **Cadence:** You choose **one pillar per generate run** — no forced rotation or schedule.
>
> **Proof / metrics:** Optional. Mention shipped work only when it fits the story; never invent numbers.

### Pillar 1: Industry Takes (PM & AI)
*For founders, product leaders, recruiters, and PM peers — timely POV on the market.*
- **Ideas:** Hot takes on AI in product (Search, agents, enterprise sales vs delivery). What a headline means for Indian startup PMs. Respectful disagreement with popular narratives. “If I were PM at X, I’d…”.
- **Tone:** Analytical, concise, opinionated — not a link dump.
- **Feed use:** Strong fit for intel feed + optional weekly synthesis themes.

### Pillar 2: AI-Native PM Craft (The "How-To")
*For founders and technical hiring managers — you build, not only write docs.*
- **Ideas:** How you approach RAG, agents, or MCP in real workflows. Prompt/PRD patterns with LLMs. What broke in v1 and what you changed. Tooling (Cursor, etc.) without tutorial fluff.
- **Proof (optional):** RAG chatbot, Review Analyzer, LLM-assisted BRD/SOW — only when relevant.

### Pillar 3: Product Ops & Delivery (The "Impact")
*For hiring managers and product leadership — messy scale and adoption.*
- **Ideas:** Intake and ownership models. Why internal tools fail adoption. Wrike/ops visibility lessons. Cross-functional delivery without heroics.
- **Proof (optional):** Automation ops engine, phased roadmaps, adoption metrics — when it strengthens the point.

### Pillar 4: PM Journey (Not Just a BA)
*For recruiters, TAs, aspiring PMs, and peers — credibility as a **new PM**, not a senior BA.*
- **Ideas:** What changed when you started operating as PM (discovery, prioritization, shipping). NextLeap / fellowship learnings. Sales + QA background as empathy, not as “still BA”. Swish-style teardowns (VoC → brief → PRD → prototype). Honest “early PM” reflections.
- **Avoid:** Defensive “I’m not a BA” rants; show PM work instead.

## 4. Networking Strategy (Pre-Phase 5)
- **Engage with Target Companies:** Start following and commenting on posts from product leaders at Series A-C Indian startups. Don't just "Like"—leave thoughtful comments that show your analytical mindset.
- **Join Communities:** Be active in the NextLeap community, The Product Folks, and relevant Slack/Discord groups for Indian PMs.
- **Build in Public:** When you update your portfolio or build a new MVP, share the *process*, not just the final link. Talk about the RICE prioritization or the prompt engineering challenges you faced.
